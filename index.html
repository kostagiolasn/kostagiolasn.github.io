Selected Publications

Mobile visual clothing search

A mobile visual clothing search system is presented whereby a smart phone user can either choose a social networking image or capture a new photo of a person wearing clothing of interest and search for similar clothing in a large cloud-based ecommerce database. The phone’s GPS location is used to re-rank results by retail store location, to inform the user of local stores where similar clothing items can be tried on.
GA Cushen, MS Nixon

Detailed Research Interests

A more detailed list of my interests includes Machine Learning Applications in Bioinformatics (e.g. Genomic Data Mining and Sequence Classification, Biomedical Imaging / Segmentation), Natural Language Processing (Dialog Systems, Neural Knowledge Language Models, Text-to-Image Models) and Computer Vision (Image Captioning, Scene Understanding).

I am also especially interested in some key theoretical aspects of Deep Learning, such as Semi-supervised Learning, Multi-modal Learning, and Bayesian Deep Learning. Lately, I have also developed a rising interest in the applications of Game Theory in Learning.

My main aim is to develop Deep Learning architectures that don’t simply specialize in just one task, but generalize well and provide solutions for many tasks, without the need of abandoning their past experience in the process of doing so. It is my firm belief that, by segmenting the neural network into semi-autonomous case-specialized sub-networks that enable/disable their learning phase independently and exchange knowledge with the other sub-networks via joint representation spaces, is a key element towards lifelong learning.
